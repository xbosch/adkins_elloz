The Weight of Time

Literary translations from Catalan, adapted for rhythm and meaning rather than word-for-word equivalence.

Breathing

In the mirror of past and present all is alive. In the instant: action. Coherence. Behind me, lived-out dreams, presents frozen in place. Ahead, possibilities waiting, untouched. On this thin line of stripped-down direction step follows step, inevitable. Now keeps growing. In each breath, time settles into layers. When air leaves the body, the trace turns fossil. Snake-skin shed. Eagle eyes.

Hands

Flayed hands. Skin gives way. Salt in every crack. Weather that wears things down. Years of service. The fingers remember without thinking. What they touch stays on the skin. Bark. Hardened hide. At every rub, more bone. The hand knows without words. It touches and lets go. What remains is not an object. It is an imprint. Under the skin, time keeps digging.
