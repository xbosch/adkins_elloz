Still Frame

Still Frame brings the series to its most distilled form. The face becomes a contained structure. The composition tightens.

The surrounding frame asserts itself as part of the image. The face no longer floats. It is held in place.

The eyes remain vertical and impersonal. The mouth stabilizes the lower field. Expression is reduced to balance rather than emotion.

Color is calmer and more controlled. Gesture remains visible but restrained. The surface carries resolution rather than tension.

This work closes the sequence. It transforms the face into a symbol.
