Faces

The Faces series reduces the human figure to its most immediate signal. There is no expression to decode and no narrative to follow. Presence replaces identity.

Each work uses simplified geometry and direct color to suggest a face without committing to portraiture. The images feel familiar yet impersonal. They resist character and avoid psychology.

Seen together, the four faces operate as a sequence. Repetition sharpens difference. Small shifts in color and proportion alter the emotional register of the whole.

Acquiring the complete set preserves this internal dialogue. The series reads as a unified statement rather than four isolated images.
